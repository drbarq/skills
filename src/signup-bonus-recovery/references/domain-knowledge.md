# Domain Knowledge — Programs, Routing, and Escalation

Reference for the facts that decide *who* can fix a dispute and *how* to frame the ask. Verify
current specifics with a web search when a case turns on exact thresholds or terms — programs
change these.

---

## Miles/points vs. status-qualifying points

| | Miles / points | Status-qualifying points |
|---|---|---|
| Examples | MileagePlus miles, SkyMiles, AAdvantage miles, Bonvoy points | United **PQP**, Delta **MQD** (formerly MQM/MQS) |
| Purpose | Redeemable for awards | Count toward elite status only |
| Who awards | Issuer transfers; loyalty receives | Loyalty program awards (issuer signals trigger) |
| On the credit card statement? | **Yes** (bonus-miles trigger line) | **No — never** |
| Resets? | No (subject to expiration policy) | **Yes — annually**, applied to a specific program year |

The asymmetry is the crux of most "half my bonus is missing" cases: the statement can only ever
evidence the miles half. The status-points half must be verified in the loyalty account, and is
credited only by the loyalty program.

---

## Who credits what

- **Issuer (Chase / Amex / Citi / Barclays):** determines spend-goal completion, transfers
  miles/points, and can re-send mis-routed transfers or correct a member number on file. The
  issuer **cannot** credit status-qualifying points.
- **Loyalty program (United / Delta / American / Marriott / Hilton):** receives miles/points,
  **awards the status-points component**, merges duplicate accounts, and backfills stranded
  balances.

**Routing rule:** missing **status points** → loyalty program is primary. Missing **miles the
issuer says it transferred** but the loyalty side never received → both, but the loyalty program
usually has to locate/credit; the issuer provides proof of transfer. Duplicate/changed account →
loyalty program performs the merge; issuer corrects the on-file number.

## Posting windows

- Typical issuer/loyalty language: **allow 6–8 weeks after the spend requirement is met** for the
  bonus to post. Some programs say 6–8 weeks after a fixed date for annual card bonuses.
- Always calculate from the **trigger cycle** (the statement where the bonus-miles line or the
  spend-goal completion appears), not from account opening.
- When the window has clearly elapsed, say so explicitly and stop accepting "please allow 6–8
  weeks" as a resolution.

## Corroborating the offer (technique)

The user's screenshots prove what posted; these prove what was **promised**:

1. **Dated third-party documentation.** Card blogs (AwardWallet, One Mile at a Time, Upgraded
   Points, The Points Guy, NerdWallet) publish offers *with publication dates*. An article dated
   within a day or two of the account-open date is strong proof the offer was live then. Many
   also maintain **welcome-offer-history** pages showing each offer period.
2. **Wayback Machine snapshot of the issuer's own offer page** near the application date — the
   strongest single artifact (issuer's own page, timestamped). Calendar view:
   `https://web.archive.org/web/<YYYYMMDD>000000*/<offer-page-URL>`; direct:
   `https://web.archive.org/web/<YYYYMMDD>000000/<offer-page-URL>`. The timestamp embedded in the
   snapshot URL is what makes it tamper-proof; have the user screenshot the page **with the URL
   bar visible**.
3. **Don't cite the issuer's current live offer** if it differs from the one applied for — a rep
   seeing a different current number gets confused. Match the evidence to the application period.

## Framing the ask (what actually moves a case)

- **Build the evidence stack, then ask for the credit — not another investigation.** A complete
  stack is: (a) offer proof dated to the application period, (b) the spend-goal trigger on the
  statement, (c) proof one half posted, (d) proof the other half demonstrably did not (the
  zeroed dashboard line / absent activity entry), (e) any in-app "you earned X" confirmation.
- **Neutralize false "it posted" claims with their own data.** The single most effective line:
  "Please tell me the specific loyalty activity-log entry or dashboard line where it appears."
  If it didn't post, they can't answer, which forces a real look.
- **Surface time-sensitivity.** Because status points reset annually, a credit must land in the
  correct program year — say this up front.
- **Keep it professional.** Firm and factual outperforms angry. Profanity or threats in the
  written record never help and can stall a case.

## Program-specific quick notes (verify current values by search)

- **United MileagePlus (Chase co-brands: Quest, Explorer, Club, Gateway):** status currency is
  **PQP**; cards also earn PQP via spend (e.g. 1 PQP per $20, capped per year) plus an annual
  Card Bonus PQP. Welcome offers frequently pair bonus miles with a one-time PQP component. PQP
  posts to the loyalty account, never the statement.
- **Delta SkyMiles (Amex co-brands):** status currency is **MQD**; some cards offer MQD
  headstarts/boosts. Miles vs. MQD split mirrors the United model.
- **American AAdvantage (Citi/Barclays):** Loyalty Points drive status; welcome bonuses are
  usually miles, sometimes with Loyalty Point implications.
- **Marriott Bonvoy / Hilton Honors (Amex/Chase):** points-only welcome bonuses plus elite-night
  credits; "missing bonus" cases are usually points or Free Night Awards, posted by the hotel
  program.

When a case hinges on a precise threshold, current offer, or term, **search to confirm** rather
than relying on these summaries — co-brand terms are revised often.
